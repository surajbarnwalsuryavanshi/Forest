The tiger is the largest living cat species

Panthera tigris

The tiger is the largest living cat species and a member of the genus Panthera. It is most recognisable for its dark vertical stripes on orange fur with a white underside. An apex predator, it primarily preys on ungulates, such as deer and wild boar.

The lion is a large cat of the genus Panthera

Panthera leo

The lion is a large cat of the genus Panthera native to Africa and India. It has a muscular, broad-chested body; short, round ears; and a hairy tuft at the end of its tail. It is sexually dimorphic; adult male lions are larger than females and have a prominent mane.
